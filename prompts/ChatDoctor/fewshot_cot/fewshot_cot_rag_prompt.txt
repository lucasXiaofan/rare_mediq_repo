Effectiveness of the Combined Contraceptive Pill and Missed Dose Guidance: The combined oral contraceptive pill is highly effective (around 99%) when taken consistently and correctly. Missing a pill by a few hours generally does not significantly reduce its effectiveness, but if a pill is missed for more than 24 hours, additional precautions should be taken. If the pill was started within the past seven days, and unprotected sex occurred, there may be a slightly increased risk of pregnancy, and additional contraception (like the morning-after pill) may be considered.
Morning-After Pill and Timing of Effectiveness: The morning-after pill is most effective when taken within 24 hours of unprotected sex, but it can still provide significant protection up to 72 hours afterward. It works by delaying ovulation and does not interfere with ongoing contraceptive use. If a dose of the regular contraceptive pill is missed and unprotected sex occurs, taking the morning-after pill can provide extra protection, especially if the pill schedule was not fully established in the first week.
Risk of Pregnancy During the First Week of Contraceptive Use: When starting the combined oral contraceptive pill for the first time, it takes about seven days for the contraceptive effect to become fully established. During this first week, additional contraception (such as condoms) is recommended. If unprotected sex occurs during this time and a pill is missed, there is an increased risk of ovulation and pregnancy, so emergency contraception may be advised. Continuing the regular pill as soon as possible is important to maintain contraceptive effectiveness moving forward.

### Instruction:
To permanently attach metal legs to a chair, you can A: Weld the metal together to get it to stay firmly in place, B: Nail the metal together to get it to stay firmly in place

### Response:
Let's think step by step.
Step 1: Consider the materials involved. Metal legs require a strong and permanent bonding method, as they are subject to weight and movement.
Step 2: Examine the options provided. Welding involves fusing the metal parts together, creating a sturdy, lasting bond. Nailing, however, is generally ineffective with metal, as nails are primarily designed for materials like wood and may not securely hold metal together.
Step 3: Analyze which method would be most suitable for a permanent attachment. Welding is the industry-standard approach for joining metal pieces in furniture and structural applications, as it provides a durable and immovable bond.
Step 4: Given that nailing is not appropriate for metal-to-metal bonding and lacks the strength required for furniture use, welding is clearly the better option.
Step 5: The answer is A: Weld the metal together to get it to stay firmly in place.
